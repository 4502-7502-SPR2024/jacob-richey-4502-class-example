7 must-visit coffee shops in Columbia

What’s a college town without coffee shops? Whether you’re looking to work, study, hang out with friends, or just enjoy a latte, these cafes are essential to the CoMo experience.

Lakota Coffee Company

With three locations to choose from, Lakota Coffee Company is as classic as it gets for local Columbia coffee spots.

Visit

Downtown location (great for studying): 24 S. Ninth St.

South location (great for hanging out): 3111 Green Meadows Way

Hospital kiosk (great for grab-and-go): 1 Hospital Drive

Vert1go Vol. 1

By Griff

Though technically an extended play, this four-song collection from British artist Griff feels cosmically sized. Swifties would celebrate Griff’s self-written and largely self-produced tracks that spell out heartbreak like poetry.

Image Credit

Stick Season

By Noah Kahan

Okay, you’ve probably heard this one. But if the title track to this 14-track record, which has 21 songs on its extended version, is all you’ve heard, put on your headphones and prepare to go on a folk journey that calls to mind Swift’s country days blended with the lyricism of her folk sister records.

Image Credit

Breaking Point

By Luís Fernandes

Another EP that snuck on this list, Breaking Point by Portuguese indie pop artist Luís Fernandes is too good not to share. It’s a marriage between stripped-down bedroom rock and 80s synth pop that Swift would be proud of.

Image Credit

Snow Angel

By Reneé Rapp

Broadway star-turned pop singer Reneé Rapp’s breakout record landed her hosting Saturday Night Live recently, and she has the discography to more than back it up. Snow Angel is an infectious record that gets better with every listen.

Image Credit

The Rise and Fall of a Midwest Princess

By Chappell Roan

A record that’s been on my music bucket list since its release, Chappell Roan’s debut album has also been stuck in my head since I listened. The experimental hyperpop might surprise fans who prefer Swift’s country days, but one listen to this album’s wrenching lyrics and you’ll be hooked.

Image Credit

Hold the Girl

By Rina Sawayama

Rina Sawayama touches on the meaning of life, activism and more in her sophomore record. A hyperpop-country-alternative pop blend, this record feels like a breath of fresh air in the music scene.
